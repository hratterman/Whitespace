# Output Specification

The deliverable is a presentation-grade strategic report in Markdown
(`report.md`), structured as a category manager's argument - a strategy deck
in document form, not a data dump. Every claim pairs with its evidence and a
committable action.

## Required structure

Write each section as a deck "slide": a headline that states the takeaway
(not the topic), then the argument and evidence beneath it. A reader skimming
only the headlines should get the whole argument.

1. **Title & why-it-matters** - one line on the money at stake and the
   thesis of the analysis.
2. **The diagnosis** - demand problem or merchandising problem, stated
   plainly, with the supporting attach/spend/mix/channel pattern
   (full-diagnostic mode) or the composition-and-merchandising read
   (public-data mode). This comes before any opportunity detail.
3. **The composition gap** - the lead analytical slide: brand mix vs
   behavior-shifting benchmark(s) by bucket, with figure types labeled.
   Purchase mix where available; catalog share labeled as offer-side
   otherwise.
4. **Channel leakage & recapture headroom** - full-diagnostic mode only.
   Capture gap in share points vs benchmark; what recapture is worth
   directionally and what merchandising/digital fix drives it.
5. **What the buyer sees today** - the merchandising/curation critique of the
   brand's own storefront experience: findability, guidance, bundling, dead
   long tail.
6. **How the leaders merchandise** - competitor benchmark: named packs,
   curation, bespoke; a short comparison table is appropriate here. Note
   where offer-presence hasn't shifted behavior (the presence ≠ behavior
   case) if the data shows one.
7. **Proposed packs** - from the existing catalog: name, target
   model/segment, buyer need/affinity it maps to, included SKUs (flag any
   item that doesn't exist yet), component-sum vs bundle price.
8. **Pricing & product-quality note** - where relevant: at/below-peer pricing
   closing the price question, and any similar-price-inferior-product flag.
9. **The recommendation, sequenced** - *Now* (committable from today's
   catalog: packs, merchandising fixes) then *Next* (new capability: new
   SKUs, bespoke programs). Each item concrete enough to commit to.
10. **Next step** - one concrete, low-cost action or open question to close
    on (a test, a data pull, a pilot).
11. **Appendix: mapping & data notes** - bucket-mapping resolutions made by
    the model (SKU → bucket + reason), all data flags carried through, and
    figure-type definitions.

## Rules

- Label every percentage with its figure type (purchase mix / catalog share /
  attach / channel share) on first use in each section.
- No unsourced figures presented as fact - carry flags through to the
  appendix and caveat inline where material.
- Halo opportunities are labeled halo and never sized as volume.
- Tables for comparisons; prose for argument. No orphan statistics - a number
  without a "so what" doesn't ship.
- Keep proprietary/licensed inputs out of any externally-shareable version
  (directional statements only).
- House style: never use em dashes or en dashes anywhere, in any
  deliverable. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or plain hyphens instead.
