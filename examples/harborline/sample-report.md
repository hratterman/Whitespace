# Harborline Fitness: Members Show Up. The Schedule Doesn't Take Them Anywhere.

*Studio programming & membership analysis · full-diagnostic mode · custom premise (`studio-schedule`) · synthetic demonstration data throughout (see Appendix)*

**Why it matters:** Harborline members book classes at nearly the benchmark rate — then visit 4.1 times a month against Ironworks' 7.6, and route 36% of bookings through revenue-sharing aggregators. Frequency is retention: part of a 3.5-visit gap is the difference between a member who renews and one who churns at month four.

## 1. The diagnosis: a programming problem, not a demand problem

| Question | Harborline | Ironworks (benchmark) | Read |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Participation** — members booking any class/month | 68% | 74% | Near benchmark — members show up |
| **Frequency** — visits per active member/month | 4.1 | 7.6 | Half the benchmark habit |
| **Mix** — share of bookings | 44% cardio | 38% strength | Comfort-zone heavy |
| **Booking capture** — own app & front desk | 52% | 71% | 36% leaks to aggregators |

Members exist and book; they just never go anywhere. No journeys, no progression, no reason to book direct. Acquiring more members would pour new names into the same shallow habit loop — the fix is programming and routing, not marketing.

## 2. The composition gap (booking mix — share of bookings, sums to 100% per studio)

Cardio/conditioning takes 44% of Harborline bookings; strength & progression — the bucket that carries Ironworks' frequency at 38% of its bookings — takes 9%. Community programming, the habit glue, is 3% vs 9%. The formats exist on Harborline's schedule (schedule share: 12% strength, offer-side); the bookings don't follow, because nothing routes members toward them.

## 3. Benchmark behavior, not presence: Pulse is the proof

Pulse Collective runs the flashiest specialty menu in the market — aerial, pole, themed rides — and its members still book 47% cardio, visit 4.4×/month, and leak 41% of bookings to aggregators. Menu breadth did not shift behavior. Ironworks shifted behavior with ordinary formats and extraordinary routing: goal journeys, next-class suggestions, coach check-ins at weeks two and six.

## 4. Booking capture: 19 points, with margin attached

Own-channel capture 52% vs Ironworks' 71% (booking share). Aggregator bookings carry a revenue share on every class and produce the loosest-held members — single-class bookers who never see a journey. The programs below are the capture play: journeys only exist in the studio's own app.

## 5. What the member sees today

A chronological week grid filtered by location. No "start here." No suggested next class — every visit is a cold start. Strength formats visually identical to everything else. And the free Community Run Club — the studio's best habit-builder — isn't listed in the app at all.

## 7. Programs from the existing schedule

Members over-index on strength training (126) and endurance events (118) (affinity index, 100 = par); dance under-indexes (84) — no dance theming, whatever competitors do. Every class already exists; one starred item is new (staff time, not construction):

- **First 30 Days Path** — new members: Intro Cycling $20 · Lifting Basics $22 · Sunrise Flow $24 · HIIT 45 $28 → components $94, **program $69**
- **Stronger in 12 Weeks** — affinity 126: Lifting Basics $22 · Kettlebell Fundamentals $26 · weekly Strength 60 $28 · *coach form check-in $35 (new)* → **$96/mo**
- **Race Ready Series** — affinity 118: Treadmill Intervals $26 · Rowing Blast $26 · Restorative Stretch $22 · Run Club free → components $74, **program $59**

## 8. Pricing: at parity; the gap is routing, not rate

Comparable formats sit within a couple of dollars of both competitors (Ride 45: $28 vs $28). Nothing in the price list explains a 3.5-visit gap. One note: the Run Club has no listed price and no app listing — free is a feature; invisible is not.

## 9. The recommendation, sequenced

**Now — today's schedule:** launch the three programs, bookable only in the own app (frequency play and capture play are the same feature); rebuild the app around journeys with a next-class suggestion after every booking; list the Run Club; give every aggregator visitor one book-direct offer.

**Next — new capability:** coach touchpoints for Stronger in 12 Weeks; individualized-programming pilot after program take-rate proves demand; add a skills/clinics bucket (absent entirely today).

## 10. Next step

Ship **First 30 Days Path** to everyone who joined in the last 90 days, instrumented for 60 days: take-rate, visits vs holdout, own-app share. In parallel, pull class-level fill rates by channel — the dataset this analysis lacked.
